New here? Read this first. The Dhaka Stock Exchange (DSE) is dominated by syndicates — groups that pump a stock up to lure retail buyers, then dump on them. So we judge every stock on two questions (the two-axis idea): 1) Is the price being manipulated? and 2) Is it a real, solvent business? A stock is "core-worthy" only when the answer is no to manipulation and yes to the business. Everything on this page serves those two questions. Because DSE prices are driven by manipulation more than fundamentals, we do not promise price targets — we focus on spotting manipulation, controlling risk, and mechanical exits.

Market context (the backdrop)

Liquidity driver: money rotates into DSE when bank deposit rates are low and out when they rise. Watch interest rates vs. deposit yields — that tide lifts or sinks most stocks.

Seasonality (a tilt, not a rule): quality names often dip to buy-able levels Jun-Aug (after the national budget); syndicates tend to launch pumps Nov-Jan and dump in Jun and Sep. Lean toward accumulating quality on summer dips and being defensive Sep-Oct.

Honest note: this is a qualitative regime read, not a live macro dashboard.
